What $10,000 invested once for 20 years looks like

Investing $10,000 as a one-off lump sum for 20 years is a striking demonstration of what patience and compound interest can achieve. At a 7% return your $10,000 almost quadruples to around $38,700 — with compounding adding nearly $29,000 on top of your original investment. At 10% it grows to $67,300, nearly seven times what you started with. For Australians who receive a $10,000 windfall and choose to invest it in their 40s, this could be worth $38,000-$67,000 by the time they reach retirement age.

Quick answer

  • A lump sum grows fastest with time — the later years matter most.
