Best Investment Tools for Australians: PocketSmith and Sharesight

Choosing the right investment tools can make managing money much easier. Two of the most useful tools for many Australians are PocketSmith and Sharesight.

These tools do different jobs, but both can be extremely helpful. PocketSmith is designed more around budgeting, cash flow and forecasting, while Sharesight is focused more on tracking your investment portfolio, returns and dividends.

Used well, they can help you become more organised, more consistent and more confident with your finances.

PocketSmith

PocketSmith is a personal finance tool that helps you understand your broader financial picture. It is especially useful for budgeting, cash flow planning and forecasting future money movements.

One of its biggest strengths is that it does not only show what has already happened. It also helps you look ahead. That can be valuable if you are trying to plan regular investing, manage savings goals or get better control of your monthly finances.

PocketSmith may suit people who want to:

  • track spending across accounts
  • manage a budget more clearly
  • forecast future cash flow
  • see a fuller view of their finances
  • plan how much money they can consistently invest

For many people, successful investing begins with managing cash flow properly. If you know what you can regularly invest without stress, long-term progress becomes much more achievable.

Try PocketSmith

Sharesight

Sharesight is an investment portfolio tracking tool. It is especially useful for investors who want to monitor how their ETFs and shares are performing over time.

Rather than focusing on day-to-day budgeting, Sharesight is more about investment visibility. It can help you better understand your portfolio returns, dividend income and overall performance.

Sharesight may suit people who want to:

  • track ETF and share holdings
  • monitor dividend income
  • see total return more clearly
  • keep investment records organised
  • measure long-term portfolio performance

This can be very useful for long-term investors because it makes portfolio progress easier to understand and review.

Try Sharesight

PocketSmith vs Sharesight

PocketSmith and Sharesight are not really direct competitors. They solve different problems.

PocketSmith is generally more useful for:

  • budgeting
  • cash flow planning
  • forecasting future finances
  • managing your broader financial life

Sharesight is generally more useful for:

  • tracking investments
  • monitoring ETF and share performance
  • viewing dividends and returns
  • keeping portfolio records organised

In simple terms, PocketSmith helps you manage the money that goes into your investments, while Sharesight helps you monitor the investments themselves.

That is why many investors may find value in using both.

Which One Is Better?

The better tool depends on what you need most right now.

If your main goal is to improve budgeting, understand your cash flow and plan your finances more effectively, PocketSmith may be the better fit.

If your main goal is to track your ETF or share portfolio and understand performance more clearly, Sharesight may be the better fit.

For some people, the best solution is not choosing one over the other, but using each for what it does best.

Final Thoughts

If you are looking for useful investment tools in Australia, PocketSmith and Sharesight are both strong options.

PocketSmith is especially useful for budgeting, forecasting and financial planning. Sharesight is especially useful for tracking investment performance, dividends and portfolio progress.

Used properly, these tools can help you make better decisions and stay more consistent over time.
