What makes a great compound interest calculator?

The best calculators allow you to:

  • Start with an initial investment
  • Add regular contributions (weekly, monthly, etc.)
  • Adjust interest rates
  • Choose compounding frequency
  • Compare two scenarios side-by-side
  • See a visual graph of growth over time
Tip: The biggest mistake people make is only testing one scenario. Comparing two scenarios is where the real insights happen.

Why comparing scenarios matters

You might want to compare:

  • $100 per week vs $500 per month
  • Starting now vs starting in 5 years
  • 6% vs 8% returns

Small differences can lead to massive changes over time due to compounding.
