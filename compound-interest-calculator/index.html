What compound interest means

Compound interest means your money can grow not only from your original amount and new contributions, but also from the returns already earned along the way. Over longer time frames, this can make a very large difference.

The calculator above is designed as an educational estimate. Real-world results can vary because of fees, taxes, inflation, market changes, contribution timing and actual investment performance.

Frequently asked questions

What annual return should I use?

That depends on what you are modelling. Many people test a few different assumptions such as 4%, 6%, 8% and 10% so they can see a range of possible outcomes rather than relying on one number.

Why compare two scenarios?

Comparing scenarios helps you understand the effect of changing one variable, such as contributing more, starting earlier, or earning a different return over time.

Does this calculator include tax, inflation or fees?

No. This calculator is for educational use and estimates growth before those real-world factors are applied.

Why does compounding frequency matter?

More frequent compounding means returns are applied more often, which can slightly increase the final balance over long periods.
